# Russian Founder GTM and First Revenue Plan

## CEO Review Verdict

The product should be built for Russian-speaking founders and owner-operators first, not for finance teams. The sharpest paid job is not "AI financial literacy" and not a generic CFO chatbot. The wedge is an independent financial second opinion for a founder who already has finance people, reports, Excel files, and uncomfortable decisions, but does not fully trust the interpretation.

The first revenue should come before a complete SaaS product: sell a paid diagnostic and owner brief, deliver it with AI-assisted analysis plus expert review, then productize the repeated parts.

## Primary Buyer

Primary buyer:

- Russian-speaking owner, CEO, COO, or commercial director.
- Company revenue: 0.5-5B RUB, with the first sweet spot around 0.8-3B RUB.
- Sectors: distribution, wholesale, e-commerce, retail, then B2B services.
- Finance function exists, but is underbuilt: accounting, financial manager, partial management reporting, weak treasury/FP&A/control.

Do not start by selling to:

- Professional CFOs who want another FP&A system.
- Accountants who need bookkeeping automation.
- Consultants who want a white-label tool.
- Early microbusinesses below the complexity threshold.

## Positioning

Short positioning:

> Independent AI-CFO second opinion for Russian-speaking founders: understand what your finance numbers really mean, what risks are hidden, and which questions to ask your finance team before you make a decision.

What the product is:

- Owner-side financial assurance layer.
- Private executive brief.
- Evidence-backed second opinion.
- Red-flag detector across liquidity, margin, CAPEX, debt, working capital, and finance maturity.
- Practical questions for the finance team.

What the product is not:

- Replacement for a CFO.
- Accounting automation.
- ERP, 1C, BI, or FP&A platform.
- Financial course.
- Generic LLM chat over uploaded Excel files.

## Why Russian-Speaking Founders Are the Right First Market

Russian-speaking founders in the 0.5-5B RUB revenue band often hit financial complexity before they build strategic finance. They may have revenue growth, receivables, inventory, discounts, credit lines, CAPEX, legal entities, and management reporting, but the founder still receives fragmented explanations.

The painful founder questions are practical:

- "Finance says cash gap is normal. Is it?"
- "Commercial team wants discounts. How much margin and cash risk are we taking?"
- "We have profit, but no money. Where is the leak?"
- "Do I need a CFO now, or first treasury, controller, and FP&A?"
- "Is my finance director explaining the business clearly or hiding behind reports?"
- "Can I trust this budget, CAPEX plan, or credit proposal?"

This is a founder trust problem, not a dashboard problem.

## Product Wedge

The MVP should behave like a private owner brief, not like a general analytics screen.

First paid artifact:

1. Executive verdict.
2. Risk radar.
3. Evidence trail.
4. Questions to ask the finance team.
5. Decision cards.
6. Finance maturity diagnosis.
7. Hiring/finance-function recommendation.

The founder should receive a concise answer:

- What changed?
- Why does it matter?
- How much money or liquidity is at risk?
- What is confirmed by data?
- What is a hypothesis?
- What should I ask my finance team?
- What decision should not be delayed?

## First Revenue Offer

Start with a productized paid service, not self-serve SaaS.

### Offer 1: Founder Financial Second Opinion

Price:

- 75-150k RUB per company for a 7-14 day diagnostic.

Input:

- BDR/P&L for 12-24 months.
- BDDS/cash flow or bank cash movement.
- Balance sheet if available.
- Receivables/payables aging.
- Inventory summary for trade/e-commerce/retail.
- Debt schedule.
- CAPEX/project plan if relevant.
- Current org chart of the finance function.

Output:

- 8-12 page owner brief.
- Top 5 red flags.
- Data quality score.
- Liquidity/margin/working-capital/debt/CAPEX read.
- Finance maturity score.
- 10 questions for the finance team.
- 30/60/90-day action plan.
- Recommendation on who to hire next in finance.

Sales promise:

> "За 7-14 дней дадим независимый второй взгляд на финансовую картину: где риск, где не хватает данных, какие вопросы задать финдиректору и какие решения нельзя откладывать."

### Offer 2: Monthly Owner Brief

Price:

- 75-300k RUB/month depending on number of legal entities, files, frequency, and expert review.

Cadence:

- Monthly for most companies.
- Weekly only for high-volatility cases: cash gap, debt restructuring, aggressive growth, margin collapse, acquisition, large CAPEX.

Output:

- One private owner brief per period.
- Red flags and changes since previous period.
- Decision backlog.
- Data freshness and missing evidence.
- Questions for CFO/finance team.

### Offer 3: Trigger-Based Review

Price:

- 100-300k RUB per event.

Triggers:

- Discount campaign.
- Large CAPEX.
- Credit line or covenant risk.
- Acquisition or sale of business unit.
- CFO hiring/change.
- Cash gap.
- Falling margin.
- Receivables or inventory spike.
- Budget approval.

This is the fastest path to high-intent revenue because the buyer already has urgency.

## First 10 Customers

The first customers should be acquired manually through trust channels.

Best channels:

- Founder communities and closed business clubs.
- Existing finance consultants and fractional CFOs who do not want to do low-level diagnostics manually.
- Accountants/1C integrators serving larger SMBs, with referral fee.
- Bank/credit brokers who see companies with weak financial packaging.
- Telegram and private founder networks, but with case-based content, not generic posts.

Outbound target:

- 100 founders/CEOs/COOs in distribution, wholesale, e-commerce, and retail.
- Filter by signals: hiring finance director, revenue 0.8-3B RUB, multiple legal entities, active debt, rapid growth, marketplace/retail expansion, inventory-heavy model.

First message:

> "Мы делаем независимый финансовый second opinion для собственников компаний 0.5-5 млрд руб. Не заменяем финдиректора и не внедряем 1С. За 7-14 дней проверяем, где в отчетности и решениях есть риск по ликвидности, марже, оборотному капиталу, долгу и CAPEX, и даем список вопросов вашей финансовой команде. Сейчас берем 5 платных пилотов по фиксированной цене."

## 30/60/90-Day Revenue Plan

### Days 0-30: Paid Manual Diagnostics

Goal:

- Sell 5 paid diagnostics.

Target revenue:

- 375-750k RUB.

What to learn:

- Which trigger sells fastest.
- Which files founders can actually provide.
- Which red flags repeat.
- Which questions founders ask after reading the brief.
- Whether they forward the brief to finance team, board, bank, or partner.

Do manually:

- File collection.
- Data cleaning.
- Expert review.
- Final owner call.

Automate only:

- Template intake.
- Metric extraction.
- Rule-based red flags.
- Brief structure.

### Days 31-60: Productized Repeatability

Goal:

- Convert 2-3 diagnostics into monthly monitoring.
- Sell 5-8 more diagnostics.

Target revenue:

- 1.0-2.0M RUB cumulative.

Build:

- Repeatable data request pack.
- Evidence trail.
- Data quality score.
- Owner brief generator.
- Case history.
- Standard red-flag engine.

Do not build yet:

- Deep 1C integrations.
- Multi-tenant enterprise admin.
- Complex self-serve billing.
- Full FP&A planning suite.

### Days 61-90: Recurring Pilot

Goal:

- 5 recurring customers.
- 10-15 completed diagnostics.

Target revenue:

- 2.0-4.0M RUB cumulative if diagnostics plus recurring monitoring both work.

Measure:

- Diagnostic to recurring conversion.
- Renewal intent after first monthly brief.
- Number of decisions influenced.
- Owner questions per brief.
- Willingness to pay for expert verification.

## Pricing Ladder

Use a simple ladder first.

| Plan | Price | Buyer Situation | Delivery |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Diagnostic | 75-150k RUB one-time | Founder has a problem or distrust | 7-14 day owner brief |
| Monitoring | 75-300k RUB/month | Founder wants monthly control | Monthly owner brief + red flags |
| Trigger review | 100-300k RUB one-time | Specific high-stakes decision | Decision card + expert review |
| Advisory add-on | Custom | Founder needs implementation help | Partner/expert channel, not core SaaS |

Do not start with cheap subscriptions. A low price will attract weak data, low urgency, and support-heavy users. The first product should be expensive enough that the founder expects a serious financial artifact.

## Product Requirements from GTM

To support first revenue, the app needs fewer generic features and more trust features.

Critical:

- Upload and manual entry for existing reports.
- Data quality score.
- Evidence trail for every material conclusion.
- Rule-based red flags.
- Owner brief export.
- Questions for finance team.
- Finance maturity and hiring recommendation.
- Expert review status: not reviewed / reviewed / needs clarification.

Defer:

- Always-on chat as the main product.
- Full BI dashboard.
- Direct integrations.
- Complex scenario modeling.
- Consultant multi-account portal.

## Key Risks

1. Founder says they want AI, but pays only for expert confidence.

Mitigation: sell "AI-assisted second opinion with expert review" first; reduce human work only after patterns repeat.

2. Data is too messy for automated conclusions.

Mitigation: data quality score becomes a feature, not a blocker. "Your reports are not decision-grade" is valuable if proven.

3. CFO sees the product as a threat and blocks access.

Mitigation: position as owner-side control and questions, not CFO replacement. Let founder invite CFO after first brief.

4. Product becomes consulting and never becomes SaaS.

Mitigation: every diagnostic must use the same intake, same evidence model, same rule engine, same brief structure, and tracked reusable red flags.

5. Legal/professional-advice risk.

Mitigation: call it second opinion and decision support, not financial, tax, legal, or investment advice. Material cases get expert review and disclaimers.

## North Star

The product wins if a Russian-speaking founder says:

> "Теперь я за 20 минут понимаю, что происходит с деньгами, какие выводы подтверждены цифрами, где финкоманда не доказала позицию, и какие три вопроса я должен задать до решения."

That is the first product to sell.
