## Market Signals Used

1. **Flex / Beacon:** owner-first, private, short, recurring brief. The product should not begin as another dashboard. It should package a scarce, high-signal owner read.
2. **Datarails:** trust comes from governed data, formulas, versions, reconciliation, and traceability. AI copy is not the moat.
3. **Pilot:** AI plus expert review is a monetizable trust layer. Expert time should be reserved for exceptions, irreversible decisions, conflict in data, or large financial impact.
4. **Russian wedge:** the first revenue motion is paid diagnostics at 75-150k RUB, then recurring monitoring at 75-300k RUB/month.

## Generated Directions

| Variant | Name | Competitive Lesson | Best For | Main Risk |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| A | Private Owner Brief | Flex Beacon | Fast paid wow moment and founder comprehension | Evidence trail may feel hidden |
| B | Evidence Workspace | Datarails | Trust, formulas, source traceability, future moat | Can feel too CFO/FP&A-heavy |
| C | AI + Expert Review Queue | Pilot | Monetizing expert verification and advisory layers | Can look like service delivery instead of SaaS |
| D | Paid Diagnostic Pipeline | Russian mid-market wedge | First revenue, data pack workflow, owner brief delivery | Less differentiated visually unless executed crisply |

## Recommendation

Use **D as the MVP spine**, with **A as the founder-facing output**, **B as the trust layer underneath**, and **C as the monetization ladder**.

The product should feel like:

> A paid, independent owner-side assurance system that turns existing company files into a verified founder brief, then escalates only high-stakes conclusions to experts.
